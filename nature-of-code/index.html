Task

Observe natural patterns and then translate them into code.

Briefing

This was the task during the excursion to the island Amrum, which unfortunately I could not attend. It was about recognizing natural patterns, systems, and behaviors in the environment, for example natural phenomena, animals, or plants. Afterwards, these observations should be translated into code, for example through taken photos or sketches. The goal was not a realistic representation in code but rather a reproduction of the recognized logic behind the patterns.

Development

After my exciting one-man excursion through Braunschweig, I decided to deal with clouds and their patterns. I took a few photos and then first occupied myself with the different types of clouds, of which there were more than I thought. I then sketched these very simply in js.

Cumulus Clouds

Cumulus clouds are the classic white, fluffy clouds that are often associated with beautiful, sunny days.

Stratus Clouds

Stratus clouds are flat, gray clouds that often cover the sky with a uniform layer and frequently bring drizzle.

Cirrus Clouds

Cirrus clouds are thin, feathery clouds that appear at high altitudes and in fair weather.

Altocumulus Clouds

Altocumulus clouds are white to gray, layered clouds that appear at mid-level altitudes in the sky.

Nimbostratus Clouds

Nimbostratus clouds are dense, dark clouds that are often associated with rain or snowfall and completely cover the sky.

After I finished this visual representation of the different types of clouds, I thought about how I could use the type of movement patterns for my project. During my observations, I found it most fascinating how the clouds moved across the sky and overlapped each other, or how the sky could be covered by clouds drifting through. The most logical idea for me was to use the clouds as a kind of brush, imitating the movement of clouds drifting across the sky and slowly covering it.

Since during my research I also observed clouds at different times of the day, I also found it exciting to include this aspect, and I integrated the changing colors of the sky and the clouds as an option on the website. My goal was to enable the most exciting patterns possible with the movement of the clouds.

Reflection

No project in this semester has caused me as many headaches as this one. Maybe it was due to the fact that I did not participate in the excursion and therefore felt a certain insecurity in the implementation process.

It was a big challenge for me to find a good balance between abstraction and recognizability. In the end, my idea could not be realized in the way I had originally planned.

For a theoretical further development, I could imagine, for example, also using the other cloud forms as painting tools.

But overall, the project showed me how one can use observations of one’s environment for design in programming, and thus coding can be not only a purely technical but also a very creative tool for interpretation.
